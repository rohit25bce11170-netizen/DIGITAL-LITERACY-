CASE STUDY PHISHING

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or credit card numbers. It typically happens through fraudulent emails, text messages, or fake websites designed to look legitimate.

How it happens (step-by-step):

The attacker creates a convincing email or message, often imitating a trusted institution like a bank or university.

The message contains urgent language, such as “Your account will be blocked unless you act now.”

A link is provided that directs the victim to a fake website resembling the real one.

The victim enters personal details, believing the site is authentic.

The attacker collects this information and uses it for financial theft or identity fraud.

Who is targeted:  
Phishing attacks usually target everyday internet users, students, employees, and even organizations. Individuals unfamiliar with cybersecurity practices are particularly vulnerable.

Consequences:  
Victims may suffer financial losses, unauthorized transactions, or identity theft. For students, consequences can include compromised academic accounts, stolen personal data, or misuse of email credentials. On a larger scale,phishing can lead to data breaches within institutions, damaging reputations and causing legal complications.

Phishing remains one of the most common cybercrimes because it exploits human trust rather than technical vulnerabilities. Awareness, cautious online behavior, and verifying sources before clicking links are essential defenses against this threat.
